# Housing Specialist Context

## Housing Supply
- Maintain **>= 80% of provincially assigned annual housing targets**
- Achieve **>= 95% regional average completion rate**
- Eliminate municipality-level underperformance:
  - Cambridge: **46.81% (2024) -> >= 80% annually**
- Sustain **100%+ overperformance buffer** in Kitchener and Waterloo

## Affordable Housing
- Reduce **community housing wait times year-over-year**
- Deliver **3,500 affordable units by 2029**
- Ensure **>= 60%** of affordable units serve very-low-to-low income households
- Reduce waitlist pressure:
  - Kitchener: **6,533 -> < 4,000 households**
  - Cambridge: **4,000+ -> < 2,500 households**
- Increase annual **non-market housing additions above current near-zero growth**

## Rental Market Balance
- Maintain **vacancy rate between 3% and 5%**
- Raise condo vacancy rate:
  - **0.8% -> >= 3%**
- Increase share of **units affordable to below-median-income renters**

## Homelessness
- Achieve **Functional Zero Chronic Homelessness by 2030**
- Reverse trend:
  - **+28% YoY growth -> negative growth**
- Reduce PATHS waitlist:
  - **630 individuals (~4+ years wait) -> < 200 individuals (< 1 year)**

## Water & Wastewater Infrastructure
- Ensure **0 development pauses due to servicing constraints**
- Complete **Water Master Plan by 2027**
- Enable **water transfer infrastructure (Cambridge -> Kitchener-Waterloo)**

## Cross-Domain Dependencies
- Housing performance must account for infrastructure constraints
- Housing outcomes affect healthcare through homelessness and aging pressures
- Housing supply affects employment and talent retention
